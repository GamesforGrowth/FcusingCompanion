What is Focusing

Focusing is a process aimed to uncover new insights regarding a situation, challenge, or a wicked problem someone face.

It was developed by the psychotherapist Eugene Gendlin from the University of Chicago. The Focusing process offers a unique way to establish a non-judging and an open approach to base a fruitful relationship with a challenge affecting people life.

Focusing emphasises that each person has an implicit inner-knowledge about any life situation. The sense of your implicit inner-knowledge regarding your whole challenge might be revealed by using your power of awareness. To discover new insights about your challenge, you are encouraged to slow down and to listen very carefully to what happened inside you after your mind has finished telling you what is already known.

What is Focusing

The practice of Focusing allows us to tap into our body wisdom and knowledge. Each situation we encounter in our daily life influences our conscious mind and our deep unconscious. Our unconscious encompasses all our past experiences stored as emotional memory, feelings and senses in our bodies. Focusing allows us to learn from this infinite stored wisdom.

Focusing is a process and a skill anyone can enjoy

Focusing can be done by yourself or in a Focusing partnership. In either way, A six steps process developed by Eugene Gendlin from Chicago University is implemented.  After watching people who failed to benefit from psychotherapy, Eugene Gendlin observed what people do during a therapy session that does help them to be healed. He gathered is findings to a set of six steps process and had started to teach it as a set of skills of awareness. 

The Focusing process

The six stages Focusing process offer simple instructions for accessing our body wisdom. Over the years this process has been further developed to a set of methodologies aiming to help the focuser to listen to the firm connection between mind and body. In a focusing session, we let the process to be slowly unfolded and new content to be revealed. The Focusing partner might give the Focuser suggestions and invitations aiming to create a safe and nonjudgmental environment. Ths kind of environment allows to focuser to talk from inside about what comes up when he put his awareness on his body and mind.

How working with Focusing Companion might help you

In a Focusing Partnership, you listen very carefully to how your body senses the issue or the situation challenging you. You will learn how to feel safe near these body senses associated with the whole situation affecting you. While you listen to and feel these body senses, you might start describing these senses. At the start, as you describe these senses most naturally, you may begin to feel comfortable even with your most challenging experiences. This place, where you are comfortable with a challenging situation, open the door to sense new understandings and see new ways forward.
